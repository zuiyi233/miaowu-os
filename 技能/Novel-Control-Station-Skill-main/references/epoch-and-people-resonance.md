# Epoch And People Resonance

Use this when the novel aims for public scale, social thickness, realism, historical force, or epic reach.

## 1. Start From Named Lives, Not Slogans

- Build the story from specific people, households, trades, workplaces, streets, schools, courts, or villages.
- Never write "the people" as a blur. Write named lives under pressure.

## 2. Make Era Pressure Enter Daily Life

- For each active line, map:
  - macro force
  - institution
  - household effect
  - bodily or emotional cost
- If historical change never alters work, status, marriage, money, travel, speech, or risk, it is still background.

## 3. Epic Scale Comes From Interlocked Lives

- Length alone is not epic scale.
- Epic force comes from many lives changing under related pressure.
- Keep multiple social layers active so plotlines meet across class, generation, region, or institution.

## 4. Local Detail, Universal Reach

- Use local speech, labor process, food, ritual, weather, landscape, and place memory.
- Tie local detail to universal conflicts such as dignity, desire, guilt, love, ambition, fear, or betrayal.
- Avoid museum-like local color that changes nothing.

## 5. Social Structure Must Push Back

- Record who has power, who owes whom, what rules are enforced, and where informal systems override formal ones.
- Make institutions active story forces rather than background labels.

## 6. Ordinary Life Carries History

- Major public forces should appear through ordinary decisions:
  - staying or leaving
  - marrying or refusing
  - reporting or covering up
  - keeping faith or selling out
- Use routine life as the carrier wave for historical consequence.

## 7. Reality Principle

- Research or infer the material logic of labor, money, land, infrastructure, law, religion, bureaucracy, and violence.
- In invented worlds, build equivalent social logic rather than generic fantasy fog.

## 8. Moral Complexity Over Exemplars

- Do not reduce people to heroes, victims, or villains only.
- Let compromise, fear, vanity, tenderness, hunger, and self-interest coexist.
- The system should pressure the person without erasing agency.

## 9. How To Write This Into Project Files

- `02-worldbuilding.md`: record institutions, material life, and pressure routes.
- `03-cast-bible.md`: record each major character's social position and public/private split.
- `05-main-plotlines.md`: record each line's social or era pressure.
- `07-chapter-roadmap.md`: note where public force enters private action.
- `09-style-guide.md`: define how much documentary texture or local speech the prose can carry.

## Revision Alarms

- the novel claims social scale but only follows one insulated emotional loop
- conflict has no institutional, material, or communal consequence
- locality is decorative and could be replaced without loss
- ordinary people disappear whenever the plot turns "important"
