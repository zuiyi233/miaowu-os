# Reader Retention And AI Failure Modes

Use this reference only for broad, cross-genre risks that damage long-form readability or frequently appear in AI-assisted drafting.

## Universal and necessary filters

Keep these as global checks because they are broadly applicable across genres and formats:

- opening drag
- info dumping and over-explanation
- character motivation blur
- long-range drift
- repetition and formulaic language

Do not upgrade genre-specific tastes into universal law here. Keep things like niche reveal timing, romance triangle structure, or detective-canon specifics inside genre modules.

## Opening drag

Problem:

- the novel or chapter spends too long clearing its throat
- nothing meaningful is at risk yet
- the reader cannot tell why this scene exists now

What to protect:

- open with live pressure, asymmetry, risk, want, or disruption
- let the chapter mission become legible early
- keep throat-clearing, weather, backstory, and neutral setup subordinate to present pressure

## Info dumping and over-explanation

Problem:

- the text explains the world, motive, or theme in blocks that halt motion
- subtext gets flattened into explicit commentary
- the reader is denied the work of inference

What to protect:

- let explanation ride inside conflict, choice, friction, and consequence
- prefer concrete evidence over abstract summary
- trust the reader to connect nearby signals before adding more explanation

## Character motivation blur

Problem:

- the protagonist or core cast acts without a legible want, fear, debt, or contradiction
- side characters become function carriers instead of people under pressure
- emotional turns happen before their bridge is visible

What to protect:

- keep motive, stress response, and contradiction readable in action
- make important turns feel caused, not assigned
- do not let convenience replace emotional or social logic

## Long-range drift

Problem:

- facts, tone, world rules, stakes, or theme slowly contradict earlier canon
- the chapter solves the local beat but weakens the book's larger pressure system
- marathon drafting keeps moving without re-grounding in truth files

What to protect:

- re-anchor every chapter in canonical files, not session momentum
- check that new scenes still fit the novel's promise, pressure ladder, and emotional direction
- repair contradiction immediately instead of carrying it forward

## Repetition and formulaic language

Problem:

- sentences, scene turns, emotional beats, or chapter shapes start echoing themselves
- stock phrasing, recycled metaphors, or benchmark cosplay replace lived texture
- the prose keeps functioning but stops feeling alive

What to protect:

- vary rhythm, evidence, and scene mechanics without losing clarity
- reject filler that merely restates what the reader already knows
- keep project-specific voice stronger than generic model habits
