# Chapter Title Method

Use this when the project uses chapter titles, when the user asks to name or rename chapters, or when a numbered-only chapter system may need to change.

Goal: make chapter titles act as controlled story signals instead of decorative summaries.

## 1. First Decide Whether This Project Needs Chapter Titles

Chapter titles are optional.

Use numbered chapters only when that better serves:

- invisibility
- speed
- relentless forward pull
- a deliberately plain presentation

Use titled chapters when titles materially add:

- hook value
- point-of-view or time/place orientation
- motif return
- tonal identity
- structural contrast between chapters

If a title adds nothing real, keep the chapter numbered.

## 2. Lock One Naming System For The Whole Book

Before naming individual chapters, decide the book-level system and record it in `00-project-overview.md` and `09-style-guide.md`.

Common systems:

- pure numbers
- number plus short title
- character or point-of-view labels
- time or place anchors
- image or motif titles
- pressure phrases
- object, clue, ritual, promise, or line-of-dialogue titles

Do not mix systems casually. A format shift is allowed only when it marks a real structural phase change and is written into project canon.

## 3. Give Each Title A Job

Each chapter title should carry one primary job and at most one secondary job:

- focus the chapter's core pressure or change
- create curiosity without stealing discovery
- orient the reader to point of view, time, or place
- return a motif, image, or theme-bearing object
- signal a special chapter mode or narrative stance

If a title is trying to do everything, it usually becomes weak.

## 4. Build The Title From The Chapter Control Card

Pull possible title anchors from:

- core goal
- key conflict
- dominant residue
- point of view
- time or place marker
- returning image or motif
- object, promise, lie, debt, or clue under pressure
- chapter-end hook

Choose the most load-bearing anchor, not the chapter's entire event list.

## 5. Generate 3 To 5 Candidates

Before locking the title, generate multiple options from different angles:

- pressure phrase
- concrete image or object
- contradiction
- charged verb plus noun
- speaker or point-of-view marker
- time or place anchor

Prefer precise nouns, active verbs, and language that sounds native to this book's voice.

## 6. Screen Candidates With Five Checks

Pick the best candidate by checking:

- chapter fit: does it still match the actual chapter center
- curiosity: does it create pull without giving away the payoff
- distinctiveness: does it avoid blending into nearby chapter titles
- voice: does it sound like this book, genre, narrator, and market mode
- consistency: does it match the project's naming system and title register

Reject titles that are spoilers, flat summaries, vague decorative riddles, or recycled grammatical shells.

## 7. Timing Rules

Use a two-stage process:

1. before drafting, record `3-5` candidates and choose a working title in the chapter control card
2. after the chapter passes structure and authenticity checks, run a final title-fit recheck and lock the final title

If the chapter's true center moved during drafting, change the title instead of defending the first idea.

## 8. Genre-Pressure Adjustments

- suspense and thriller chapters often benefit from pressure, threat, false certainty, or charged-object titles
- mystery chapters often benefit from clue, witness, question, or location framing
- romance chapters often benefit from emotional debt, misread promise, or relational shift framing
- literary chapters often benefit from image, contradiction, or motif return
- historical and fantasy chapters often benefit from institution, place, ritual, or cost framing

These are pressure preferences, not templates.

## 9. Anti-Patterns

- title summarizes the chapter's climax or reveal
- title sounds dramatic but the chapter does not support it
- title is generic enough to fit twenty other chapters
- title system changes every few chapters with no structural reason
- title carries information the chapter itself never earns
- title is frozen before drafting and never rechecked after the chapter changes

## 10. Default Recommendation

When no special project constraint says otherwise:

- use chapter titles only if they add real value
- keep one stable naming system for the whole book
- let each title do one main job
- generate several candidates from the control card
- finalize the title only after the accepted chapter exists

## 11. 中文执行模板

在需要直接执行章节起名时，可直接使用下面这段模板：

```text
如果这本书尚未确定是否使用章节名，先判断章节名是否真的有增益；若章节名不能提升钩子、聚焦、定位、意象回收或叙述声音，就保留纯编号。若本书使用章节名，先读取 00-project-overview.md、07-chapter-roadmap.md、09-style-guide.md 和本章 chapter control card，确认全书命名体系、标题 register、标题主职责。然后从本章的 core goal、key conflict、dominant residue、point of view、motif/object/clue、chapter-end hook 中提取最承重的 1 到 2 个命名锚点，按不同角度生成 3 到 5 个候选标题。每个标题只承担 1 个主任务，最多兼顾 1 个副任务：制造悬念、聚焦压力、提供时间/地点/视角定位、回收意象、突出本书声音。优先使用具体名词、主动动词、带压力的短语；避免剧透高潮、复述流水账、空泛文艺句、伪诗意迷雾、模板化词壳和与全书体系不一致的格式。随后按 5 项标准筛选：1. 贴章度，2. 悬念度，3. 辨识度，4. 声音匹配度，5. 体系一致性；选出一个 working title 写入章节控制卡。正文成稿并通过结构、真实性、连续性检查后，再复核标题是否仍然准确对应本章真正的 mission、turn、residue 和 ending hook；如果本章重心已经变化，立即改题，不要硬保留旧题。最终输出时给出：本书标题体系一句话、本章 3 到 5 个候选标题、每个候选标题的命名理由、最终定稿标题，以及一句不超过 20 字的定稿说明。
```
