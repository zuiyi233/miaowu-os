# Critical Evaluation Standards

Use this when reviewing project design, chapter plans, or drafted chapters. Treat each section as a gate, not a suggestion.

## 1. Causality And Narrative Load

Pass if:

- major events arise from prior pressure
- each scene changes future possibility, not just page count

Fail if:

- scenes are exchangeable
- twists arrive from outside the story's own pressure
- exposition does not alter risk

Repair:

- add the missing precondition
- turn summary into consequence
- connect the beat to an earlier debt, promise, or wound

## 2. Point Of View Discipline

Pass if:

- the reader can tell whose limits define the scene
- access to information matches the chosen distance

Fail if:

- knowledge leaks for convenience
- emotional emphasis belongs to no stable center of perception

Repair:

- tighten the camera around one perceiving center
- move stray information into action, dialogue, or a later reveal

## 3. Character Autonomy And Contradiction

Pass if:

- actions grow from desire, fear, shame, debt, contradiction, and pressure
- the character could surprise the outline without becoming false

Fail if:

- the character exists only to advance plot or theme
- people become single-trait mouthpieces

Repair:

- restore the character's conflicting motives
- add the cost of their choice to the scene

## 4. Polyphony And Counter-Pressure

Pass if:

- important scenes contain real counter-logic, not only agreement or villain noise
- competing values speak through distinct characters, classes, institutions, or generations

Fail if:

- one worldview wins because all alternatives are weak
- dialogue collapses into author sermon

Repair:

- strengthen the opposing position
- let institutions, class, family, or history answer back

## 5. Image, Motif, And Return

Pass if:

- recurring image or motif changes meaning under pressure
- the return deepens theme, memory, or irony

Fail if:

- the image is decorative
- motifs repeat without transformation

Repair:

- tie the motif to a choice, wound, or reversal
- cut ornamental recurrence

## 6. Language Vitality

Pass if:

- the sentence carries scene work, perception, or emotional force
- diction fits the novel's social and psychological world

Fail if:

- prose sounds inflated, dead, generic, or fake-profound
- abstraction replaces observation

Repair:

- replace abstraction with seen, heard, handled detail
- cut any sentence that only announces significance

## 7. Formal Necessity

Pass if:

- structure and chronology reveal truth unavailable in a simpler form
- the reader's effort is repaid with stronger meaning or pressure

Fail if:

- nonlinear movement exists for prestige effect
- fragmentation hides weak causality

Repair:

- justify each formal move by what it uncovers
- flatten the structure where no extra truth is gained

## 8. Social And World Credibility

Pass if:

- power, money, labor, custom, law, belief, and institutional pressure affect action
- the world pushes back on desire

Fail if:

- setting is backdrop only
- the novel claims scale while floating free of social mechanics

Repair:

- add the missing material or institutional consequence
- show how public force enters private life

## 9. Thematic Afterlife

Pass if:

- the chapter leaves a live question, burden, or residue
- the ending direction grows more inevitable and more costly

Fail if:

- the text says what it means without dramatic residue
- a chapter closes without altered meaning

Repair:

- move theme from statement into consequence
- end on changed obligation, not empty flourish
