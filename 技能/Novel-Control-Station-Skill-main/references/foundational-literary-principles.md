# Foundational Literary Principles

Use this at project launch, major revision, and high-level review.

Goal: turn high-signal literary craft into enforceable writing actions rather than decorative advice.

## 1. Human Truth Before Plot Convenience

- Every decisive beat must answer:
  - who wants what now
  - what fear, shame, debt, or contradiction blocks them
  - why this move is the least false move available
- If a beat exists only because the outline wants it, repair character pressure before drafting.
- Store these pressures in `03-cast-bible.md` and `05-main-plotlines.md`.

## 2. Readable Surface, Deep Structure

- Complexity is allowed only when the reader can still track:
  - present objective
  - present risk
  - present emotional debt
- Any nonlinear move, multi-line cut, or structural jump must reveal new character truth, thematic pressure, or causal relation.
- If a passage needs explanation outside the text to be legible, simplify the surface first.

## 3. Causal Pressure, Not Event Pile

- Convert every line from "and then" into "because," "therefore," or "despite that."
- Each chapter must change at least one line's risk profile, power balance, or emotional debt.
- If two chapters can switch order without damage, causality is weak.

## 4. Desire, Fear, Shame, Debt

- For every core character record:
  - visible desire
  - hidden need
  - fear
  - shame point
  - owed or unpaid debt
  - contradiction
- In each major scene, activate at least one of these forces.
- Reject scenes where characters exist only to explain, hint, or trigger plot.

## 5. Concrete Reality And Material Detail

- Anchor major scenes in sensory, labor, institutional, or procedural detail.
- Fantastic, symbolic, or elevated material still needs cost, routine, and friction.
- Prefer one precise world-bearing detail with consequence over decorative description.

## 6. Theme As Pressure

- Theme lives in choices, omissions, and cost, not in summaries.
- Define:
  - central question
  - opposing values
  - recurring wound or test
- Each chapter should pressure one value collision through action or refusal.
- Remove slogan-like lines that replace dramatized conflict.

## 7. Formal Necessity

- Point of view, tense, chronology, and chapter order must earn their complexity.
- Ask:
  - why this voice
  - why this order
  - what truth becomes visible only this way
- If the answer is only style or novelty, simplify.

## 8. Language Health

- Cut dead, inflated, fake-profound, or slogan-like prose.
- Prefer concrete nouns, active verbs, image with consequence, and syntax that matches emotional temperature.
- Keep lyrical language only if it sharpens perception, tension, or motif.

## 9. Ending Pressure

- Know the ending's emotional and moral residue before long drafting.
- Plot mechanics may stay open, but the direction of cost and meaning may not.
- Every active line should point toward consequence rather than endless extension.

## 10. Controlled Discovery

- Discovery is allowed inside known constraints.
- When drafting reveals a better truth:
  - update `08-dynamic-state.md`
  - repair every impacted structural file
  - log what changed
- Never let improvisation outrun the documents.

## Refusal Signals

- twist with no prior pressure
- beautiful paragraph doing no narrative work
- theme explained instead of enacted
- worldbuilding detached from labor, money, law, ritual, or bodily consequence
- chapter hook that creates noise instead of obligation
